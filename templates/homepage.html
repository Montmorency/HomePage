I am a Post-Doctoral Research Assistant at King's College London working for Alessandro De Vita. My current research focuses on fracture processes in hydrogen embrittled steels . In particular we are focusing on hydrogen occlusion and the effects of strain on grain boundaries in Iron and Steel. Formerly I worked on the optical properties of materials in Feliciano Giustino's group .

In my spare time I fool around with software. Mainly in the domain of python applications based on Flask/Peewee, some Haskell, and building Neural Networks using TensorFlow for a range of applications. Some of my programming interests include first principles calculations of the properties of materials, natural language processing, and market forecasting. If you are interested in collaborating on a project in one or all of these (very) disparate fields, or would like to outsource some development please don't hesitate to contact me.
