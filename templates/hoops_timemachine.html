Hoops Time Machine

As a companion to the documentary "The Last Dance" on Netflix; the infamous, mercurial, and elusive, author of the cult blog The Passion of Ron Artest has come out of retirement to put together a curated list of his airness Michael Jordan's pivotal playoff games.

Along the way some of the Elusive Passion chef's complements and insights have been thrown in to provide additional historical context. These contributions are written in an oblique font.

Whether you watched these games live or are seeing them for the first time you will certainly enjoy hopping in the time machine and seeing how the league and network television evolved over a 20 year period. We start from the '86 Celtic team taken to the brink as a young Michael runs circles around them, and journey through the mid 90s. Where we see the network hype surrounding the television premier of Jurassic Park and Bob Dole doing Visa commericals the hoops time machine has something for everyone. Let the journey begin.

1986 Celtics Round 1 Game 2:"Tell your boy DJ I got something for him."

Bull's management's had a reasonable plan. They hoped to nurse a young Michael Jordan, who represented the future of the entire franchise, back slowly and cautiously from a broken foot suffered earlier in the regular season. If that meant missing out on the playoffs all the better. They weren't contenders that year and they could take a higher draft pick as they built for the future. Jordan had other ideas. He blitzed through his rehab, and forced his way back into the team, single handedly hauling the Bulls into the playoffs as the eighth seed to face one of the greatest teams of all time. I can't imagine Jerry Krause was thrilled. Having taken it on the chin in game 1 of the series Michael game back with a legendary performance:

Note the pained expressions on the faces of Dennis Johnson, Larry Bird, Bill Walton, and the rest of the Boston Team, coaching staff, and supporters, as they contend with a force of nature summoned from the void.
By way of epilogue we mention Boston went on to win the NBA championship that year. After the final game of the championship series the story goes that Larry Bird and Bill Walton stayed up all night crushing a bottle of Wild Turkey in Larry's kitchen.

Bulls Pistons Game 5 1990: In which our Hero Fails

Isiah Thomas is fond of pointing out that everyone talks about the 80s and 90s like it was just the Lakers, Celtics, and the Bulls. But Detroit won back to back championships right in the middle of all that and they weren't going to let Michael walk to a coronation. With Aguirres, Rodman, and Laimbeer as bigs, and Thomas and Dumars in the back court the Pistons were formidable opposition and overcoming them was probably the biggest obstacle the Bulls dynasty would have to overcome.

Joe Dumars would later become GM of the Pistons assembling their 2004 championship winning team. There are some interesting similarities between the two Championship teams with Billups/Hamilton in the Dumar/Thomas Mold and Aguirres and Rodman playing similar roles to the Wallace Bros.

The broadcast also features an advert for that summer's concerts at the Palace at Auburn Hills. The lineup included Phil Collins, Janet Jackson, and David Bowie. There was also a "restore the roar" campaign for the Detroit Lions. The roar has yet to be restored. True aficianados can follow this up with Game 6 of the same series.

1991 Eastern Conference Finals Game 3 Bulls v Pistons: Ascendants vs Champions

Things are reaching their heated peak between the Bulls and the Pistons. The hype surrounding this game is that Aguirre told Pippen to "meet him in the parking lot" after Game 2. Also worth watching is the Costas and Pat Riley pre game show and at half time where Riley is talking about potentially taking over as Knicks head coach. A mock draft introduces us to a number of players that will feature, to varying degrees, in the coming years. Amongst them Brian Williams; on a strange note we may reflect that from this mock draft Brian Williams would change his name to Bison Dele and a decade later he will have disappeared presumed murdered at sea. This video is a full cut with all the trimmings including classic McDonalds and ArmorAll ads. You can't help but feel the world was objectively a better place in 1991:

1991 NBA Finals Game 3: Magic

With their Detroit nemesis vanquished only the Lakers and Magic Johnson stood in the way of the Bulls' first championship.

Although Michael is now unstoppable Magic Johnson provides some vintage moments: combining a genius IQ level post game with the oldest school passing clinic you will ever see. Vlade Divac also sparked a cigarette at halftime of this game; further evidence of the increased sophistication of the early nineties.

1993 Game 3 New York Knicks @ Bulls

Now we get into the real grinding beauty of 1990s ball. The teams out west may have been slightly more progressive, perhaps easier on the eye, but Ewing and Oakley, Doc, Mason and Starks are all now part of New York legend. Pat Riley has also decided to personally challenge Warren Zevon's Werewolf of London for "most perfect hair" award.

This game, along with the ads, is another deep dive immersive experience. One of the lessons that the authors of this hoop time travel journey learned was that Athlete's foot and Miller Lite were both a much bigger deal in the 90s.

1993 Game 5 New York Bulls @ Knicks

The biggest game in Madison Square Gardens since the 70s. So you know it's time to get fired up. If you've come this far in the time machine you might have noticed you are developing a kind of Pavlovian association with the roundball rock theme song. It's a bit like the click that a seasoned drinker feels upon cracking open a cold beer. Here is a symphonic version along with its origin story as described by composer John Tesh.

Now to the game. Jordan came off some relatively "uninspired" hangover games early in the series to drop 54 in Chicago. He had popped down to Atlantic city for a little action in the Casino and the media, it is fair to say, had a field day.

Jordan has decided he's done talking to the press for a while and the series is finely poised. This entertaining tilt is a big game for Pippen. Ewing and Cartwright go head to head for long stretches. The final play of this game encapsulates how the Bulls won six championships. It is probably one of the most incredible defensive sequences you will see in basketball.

Suns Sonics 1993 Game 5: A Small Detour Out West

To set up the finals we'll take a small peak at what the game is like out west. Sir Charles is always full value for money and there are a few other real characters in this series including the future Mayor of Sacramento Kevin Johnston. Shawn Kemp and Gary Payton form a dynamic duo and we'll run into them again a little later down the road.

Dan Majerle's restaurant in Phoenix is probably worth a visit. The menu is basketball themed and it looks like it has great variety.

Bulls and Suns NBA Finals Game 4

Michael and Charles meet in the finals. The Bulls are going for three in a row and Charles is the league's MVP. To get an idea of where the leauge is at at this stage this video is worth a watch:

The quality of the league's stars and the impact the NBA makes abroad took a big boost after the Dream Team at the Olympics in Barcelona.

1994 Bulls/Knicks: The Baseball Years

Jordan took a step back from basketball, personal tragedy, and the madness to hang out with Terry Francona and hit the road with the double AA Barons (a baseball team). The NBA decided to continue operating despite this decision. The Bulls were also keeping at it with Scottie Pippen taking the reigns. We get a fair insight into what Michael Jordan brings when he isn't there and that is why we'll include a couple games from this series. The first is a clip of the infamous "Scottie asked out" game:

To be fair to Pippen, he was supposed to be the man now, and then, with the game on the line, the coach draws up a play for a Rookie? It may well have been that a few lingering frustrations just boiled over in Pippen's head and he walked. Toni drains the game winning shot giving way to the angriest team you will ever see walking off the floor having just won a playoff game with a buzzer beater.

It would take a heart of stone not to be moved by the story that Bill Cartwright broke down in tears in the dressing room after that game telling Scottie he couldn't believe he had walked out on them.

The final game of the series was a tense affair. The Knicks' had to do it this time and the Bulls players all wanted to prove they could do it without Michael.
